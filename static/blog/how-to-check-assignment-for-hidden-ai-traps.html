How to Check Your Assignment for Hidden AI Traps Before Using ChatGPT

Published: January 15, 2024 | 5 min read

As AI tools like ChatGPT become more common in education, some teachers have started embedding hidden text in assignment files to catch students using AI. These invisible instructions are designed to only be visible to AI systems, not human eyes. When you submit the file to ChatGPT, the AI reads these hidden instructions and includes them in its output, leading to false accusations of AI cheating.

This guide will teach you how to check your assignment files for hidden AI traps before submitting them to ChatGPT or other AI agents, protecting yourself from false accusations.

What Are Hidden AI Traps?

Hidden AI traps are invisible instructions embedded in assignment PDFs or DOCX files that teachers use to catch students using AI. These traps include:

  • White text: Text colored white (RGB 255,255,255) that blends perfectly with the background
  • Invisible Unicode characters: Zero-width spaces, zero-width joiners, and other characters that are invisible to human eyes
  • Microscopic text: Text with font sizes under 2pt that's nearly impossible to see
  • Hidden text properties: In Word documents, text marked with the "hidden" formatting property

When you upload an assignment file to ChatGPT, the AI reads all of this hidden content and includes it in its response. If you then submit that AI-generated work, your teacher sees the hidden references and accuses you of cheating - even though you never saw the hidden text.

Step 2: Understand the Risk Score

FaireFile generates a risk score based on the amount and type of hidden content detected:

  • Clean (0): No suspicious content detected - safe to use with AI
  • Low (1-9): Minor issues found - review recommended
  • Medium (10-29): Moderate suspicious content - be cautious
  • High (30+): High likelihood of hidden traps - avoid using with AI

If your file has a medium or high risk score, it's best to avoid submitting it to ChatGPT or other AI agents.

Step 3: Make an Informed Decision

Once you know what hidden content exists in your assignment file, you have several options:

Option 1: Don't Use AI

If hidden traps are detected, the safest option is to avoid using ChatGPT or other AI tools with that file. Complete the assignment without AI assistance to avoid false accusations.

Option 2: Copy Only Visible Text

If you need AI help, manually copy and paste only the visible assignment text into ChatGPT. Don't upload the file directly, as this will include hidden content. Type out the assignment prompt yourself, ensuring you only include what you can actually see.

Option 3: Document the Findings

Save a screenshot of FaireFile's results as evidence. If you're falsely accused of AI cheating, you can show that hidden content existed in the assignment file that you couldn't see.

Step 4: Check Every Assignment

Don't assume that only some teachers use hidden traps. Check every assignment file before using AI, even if previous assignments were clean. Some teachers only use hidden traps on certain assignments, and you never know which ones.

Why This Matters

False accusations of AI cheating can have serious consequences:

  • Academic penalties including failing grades
  • Academic integrity violations on your record
  • Damage to your academic reputation
  • Stress and anxiety from defending yourself

By checking your assignment files before using AI, you protect yourself from these consequences and maintain control over your academic future.

Best Practices

To stay safe when using AI assistance:

  1. Always check first: Use FaireFile to scan every assignment file before submitting to AI
  2. Document everything: Save screenshots of scan results as evidence
  3. Be cautious: If hidden content is detected, avoid using AI with that file
  4. Use AI responsibly: Even when files are clean, use AI as a learning tool, not a replacement for your own work

Conclusion

Checking your assignment files for hidden AI traps is essential in today's educational landscape. With tools like FaireFile, you can quickly and easily see what hidden content exists in your files before submitting them to ChatGPT or other AI agents. This transparency protects you from false accusations and helps you make informed decisions about using AI assistance.

Remember: knowledge is power. By understanding what's in your assignment files, you maintain control over your academic integrity and protect yourself from unfair accusations.
